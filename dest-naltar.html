Naltar Valley

📍 Gilgit District, GB📏 Altitude: 2,900m (9,514 ft)🕐 2–3 Days Ideal

About Naltar Valley

Naltar Valley is one of Gilgit Baltistan's most enchanting and surprisingly underrated destinations. Located just 40 km north of Gilgit city at an altitude of around 2,900 metres, the valley is renowned for its three remarkably colourful lakes — Naltar Lake, Bashkiri Lake, and Langar Lake — each displaying a different stunning shade of blue, turquoise, or green depending on the season and light.

The valley is blanketed in one of the densest pine and fir forests in all of GB, creating a lush, cool atmosphere quite distinct from the barren rocky landscapes elsewhere in the region. The Pakistan Air Force maintains a ski resort in Naltar, making it the country's premier winter sports destination when snow blankets the valley from December to March.

In summer, Naltar offers superb hiking through the forest, excellent trout fishing in its crystal streams, and jaw-dropping wildflower meadows above the tree line. The combination of forest, lakes, and high mountain backdrop makes it one of the most photographically diverse destinations in the region.

Top Attractions & Activities

  • 💧
    Three Colored Lakes – Naltar Lake, Bashkiri Lake, and Langar Lake display extraordinary shades of turquoise, blue and green. A 2–3 hr hike leads to the upper lakes.
  • ⛷️
    PAF Ski Resort – Pakistan's best ski resort operates here December–March. Equipment rental and basic lodging is available at the resort.
  • 🌲
    Pine Forest Walks – Miles of trails wind through ancient pine and cedar forests — perfect for gentle walks, birdwatching, and picnics.
  • 🎣
    Trout Fishing – The Naltar streams are famous for brown and rainbow trout. Fishing rods can be rented locally.
  • 🌸
    Alpine Meadows – Hike above the tree line in July–August to discover high-altitude meadows bursting with wildflowers.

Travel Tips

🚗
Getting There

Naltar is 40 km from Gilgit. Drive 25 km to Nomal, then 15 km of rough road requiring a 4x4 vehicle.

🌡️
Temperature

Considerably cooler than Gilgit. Even summer nights are cold. Bring a warm jacket.

🏕️
Camping

Camping near the lakes is allowed and magical. Bring your own tent and supplies from Gilgit.

🗓️
Winter Visit

Winter visitors (Dec–Feb) can enjoy snow sports. The valley is completely transformed under snow.
